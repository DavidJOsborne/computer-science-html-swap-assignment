Why are cats so popular on the internet?

Cat pictures, videos, and memes are some of the most common and popular posts on the internet, making about 15% of the internets traffic. But why cats? What about them are so special? Why do they perform so well around all parts of the internet?

To start, they're cute.

Cats work so well on the internet because they cause an easy and repeatable reaction in humans: joy, warmth, or "kawaii" (The human reaction to something cute). Today, much of the internet's content is discovered through algorithms. Many of these algorithms push content that evokes a reaction in users, and cats are the easiest way to make an inoffensive reaction.

They're relatable too!

About 30% of households worldwide have cats. That percent increases if you only take internet users. Most of the internet understands cats and their patterns. Cats also easily display common emotions and situations, making them work as great vessels of relatablility.

Ease of Transfer

Cats work in a medium that is able to be shared and viewed very easily. Short videos and images are able to be sent all over the internet. People don't need audio to watch, and they don't need to spend time reading anything. Cats are inoffensive, and due to their relatablility, can be sent to anyone: friends, family, and coworkers.
